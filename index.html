1. Company Income Tax -CIT:
Amendment to Section 9 of the principal Act (S.2 of the Finance Act)
Charge of Tax
Interest and Dividend in subsection 1(c) of the principal Act are defined.
  • Interest shall include compensating payments received by a Borrower from its approved agent or a Lender in a Regulated Securities Lending Transaction provided that the underlying transaction giving rise to the compensating payment is a receipt of interest by a Lender on the collateral it received from its approved agent or a Borrower in a Regulated Securities Exchange Transaction.

  • dividend shall include compensating payments received by a Lender from its approved agent or Borrower in a Regulated Securities Lending Transaction if the underlying transaction giving rise to the compensating payment is a receipt of dividends by a Borrower on any shares or securities received from its approved agent or a Lender in a Regulated Securities Lending Transaction.
Substitution for Section 10 of the principal Act (S.3 of the Finance Act)
Identification of a company
  • Taxpayer Identification number (TIN) to serve as the means of identifying companies instead of incorporation number provided for in the principal Act.

  • Banks shall require all companies to provide their TIN before opening accounts.

  • Accounts already opened without TIN are given a grace period of 3 months from passage of this Act to provide their TIN

Amendment of Section 16 of the principal Act (S.5 of the Finance Act
Insurance companies
  • Insurance companies can now carry forward their losses indefinitely like other companies.

  • Minimum tax for non-life is now 0.5% of gross premium. [S.16(12a)]

  • Minimum tax for life insurance is now 0.5% of gross income. [S.16(12b)]

  • reserve for unexpired risks is to be calculated on a time apportionment basis of the risks accepted in the year. [S.16(8a)]

  • Reserve for outstanding claims and outgoings shall be an amount equal to the total estimated amount of all outstanding claims and outgoings, provided that any amount not utilised towards settlement of claims and outgoings shall be added to the total profits of the following year. [S.16 (8b)].

Amendment of Section 19 of the principal Act (S.7 of the Finance Act)
Payment of dividend by a Nigerian company
    The Finance Act has introduced exemptions to the provision of excess dividend tax in a new sub-section 2 as follows:
  • (a) Dividends paid out of the retained earnings of a company.

  • (b) Dividends paid out of profits that are exempted

  • (c) profits or income of a company that are regarded as franked investment income under this Act; and

  • (d) distributions made by a Real Estate Investment Company to its shareholders from rental income and dividend income received on behalf of those shareholders.

Amendment of Section 29 of the principal Act (S.12 of the Finance Act)
Basis for computing assessable profit
    The commencement and cessation rules are eliminated as assessments are generally on preceding year basis now.
  • The 1st year under commencement shall be assessed from the date the company commenced to the end of its first accounting period (S.29(3a))

  • Second year shall be from the day after the 1st accounting period to the end of the second period etc.

  • For cessation of business, the assessable profit in the year of cessation shall be the profit from the beginning of the accounting period to the date of cessation and the tax shall be payable within 6 months of cessation.

Amendment of Section 29 of the principal Act (S.12 of the Finance Act)
Minimum Tax
    the minimum tax to be levied and paid shall be 0.5% of gross turnover of the company, less franked investment income. (S.33(2a))

    Exemptions
  • (a) a company carrying on agricultural trade or business as defined in subsection (9) of section II, of this Act

  • (b) a company that earns gross turnover of less than twenty-five million naira in the relevant year of assessment

  • (c) any company for the first four calendar years of its commencement of business.

Substitution for Section 40 of the principal Act (S.16 of the Finance Act)
Rate of Tax

    Section 40 of the CIT Act is repealed and replaced with a new Section 40 as follows:

    There shall be levied and paid for each year of assessment in respect of total profits of every company, tax as follows.

  • In the case of

  • (a) small company, tax as provided under Section 23(1)(o) of this Act (i.e CIT at 0%).

  • (b) medium-sized company, tax at the rate of twenty kobo for every naira; and

  • (c) large company, tax at the rate of thirty kobo for every naira.

Amendment of Section 77 of the principal Act (S.18 of the Finance Act)
Time within which tax (including provisional tax) is to be paid

    There is no more payment of provisional tax as S.77(1) has been repealed.

    Every Company shall make payment of tax due on or before the due date of filing, in one lump sum or in instalments; Provided that, where the taxpayer pays in instalments:

  • a. The taxpayer shall first write, with evidence of payment of the first instalment, and obtain the approval of the Service to pay in such number of instalments as may be approved by the Service.

  • b. The final instalment must be paid on or before the due date of filing.

    • Bonus

  • Where a company pays its tax 90 days before the due date as provided under Section 55 of this Act, such company shall be entitled to a bonus of:

  • (a) 2%, if such company is a medium-sized company; and

  • (b)1% for any other company; on the amount of tax paid, which shall be available as a credit against its future taxes.

Amendment of Section 105 of the principal Act (S.22 of the Finance Act)
Interpretation

    Section 105(1) of the CIT Act is amended as follows:

  • a. Deleting the definition of "Board" and defining the term "Service" as follows: Service" means the Federal Inland Revenue Service as defined in the Federal Inland Revenue Service (Establishment) Act, 2007;

  • b. Replacing all references to "the Board" in the CIT Act with "the Service".

  • c. Providing a definition for the following terms:

  • "Large company" means any company which is not a small or medium-sized company.

  • "Medium-sized company" means a company that earns gross turnover greater than N25,000,000 but less than N100,000,000;

  • "Small company" means a company that earns gross turnover of N25,000,000 or less;

Amendment of the schedules (S.23 of the Finance Act)
    The Finance Act introduced a new schedule 7 after the sixth schedule as follows:
  • Where a Nigerian company, or a fixed base of a foreign company in Nigeria, incurs any expenditure by way of interest or of similar nature in respect of debt issued by a foreign connected person, the excess interest thereon shall be a disallowable deduction for the purpose of this Act:

  • For the purposes of sub-section (1), the excess interest shall mean an amount of total interest paid or payable in excess of thirty per cent (30%) of earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization of the Nigerian company in that accounting period.

  • Where for any assessment year, the interest expenditure is not wholly deducted against income, so much of the interest expenditure as has not been deducted, shall be carried forward to the following assessment year or assessment years, provided that no interest expenditure shall be carried forward under this sub-section for more than five (5) assessment years immediately succeeding the assessment year for which the excess interest expenditure was first computed.

FINANCE ACT 2019

On 13th January, 2020, President Muhammadu Buhari signed the Finance Bill into law to be known as the Finance Act 2019.

The objectives of the Finance Act include:
  • Promoting fiscal equity by mitigating instances of regressive taxation
  • Reforming domestic tax laws to align with global best practices
  • Introducing tax incentives for investment in infrastructure and capital markets
  • Supporting micro, small and medium sized businesses in line with the administration’s ease of doing business reforms.
  • Raising revenue for Federal, State and Local Governments.

The Act seeks to reform 7 tax laws to promote fiscal balance, provide incentives to business and raise government revenues. Expedited consideration and hearing by the National Assembly.

Shift from direct tax to indirect tax

a. Raise Revenue
  • VAT rate increase to 7.5%
  • Harness digital economy
  • Excise Duty on imports
  • Withholding tax on petroleum profit
b. Mitigate regressive rules
  • Excess dividends tax revised
  • Tax on interim dividends removed
  • Minimum tax rule changed
  • Tax rules for new and exiting business revised

Potential increase in corporate income

a. Incentivize business
  • MSMEs’ preferential income tax rates
  • Registration threshold and VAT exemption for MSMEs
  • Early CIT payment incentive
  • CGT exemption for loss of employment compensation
b. Reform the law
  • Thin capitalization rule introduced
  • Creates framework for security lending transaction
  • Pass-through status for REICs
  • Anti-avoidance provision against artificial business restructuring

Arrangement of Sections

The Finance Act consists of 57 sections analysed as follows: 1. Companies Income Tax Act - 22 sections 2. Petroleum Profits Tax Act -1 section 3. Personal Income Tax Act - 8 sections 4. Value Added Tax Act - 15 sections 5. Customs and Excise Tariff Act -1 section 6. Capital Gains Tax Act - 3 sections 7. Stamp Duties Act - 5 sections 8. Sections 1 and 57 are introduction and citation respectively.

Commencement date

  • The commencement date of the Finance Act 2019 is 13th January, 2020.
  • However, the Honorable Minister of Finance, Budget and National Planning had announced that the new VAT rate of 7.5% takes effect on 1st February, 2020.
  • Therefore, while the commencement date remains 13th January, 2020, the implementation date for the new VAT rate is 1st February, 2020.

    7. VALUE ADDED TAX (VAT)
    Relevant law

    VAT Act LFN 2004 as amended.

    VAT is chargeable on the supply of taxable goods and services except items specifically stated as exempt or zero-rated.

    Taxable Persons [S 46]

    A taxable person means an individual or body of individuals, family, corporations sole, trustee or executor or a person who carries out in a place an economic activity, a person exploiting tangible or intangible property for the purpose of obtaining income therefrom by way of trade or business or a person or agency of government acting in that capacity.

    Rate [S 4]

    7.5% of the value of goods and services

    8. PERSONAL INCOME TAX (PIT)
    Rates
    [Schedule 6]

    Personal Income Tax (PIT) is a tax levied on individuals including employees, partners in a partnership, unincorporated trust, joint ventures, families and communities. It is imposed based on source and residency rules.

    PIT rate is applied on a graduated scale and taxable income bands as set out below:

    Minimum tax [S 37, schedule 6]

    Every taxable person is liable to a minimum income tax of 1% of their gross income. This is triggered where actual tax payable after all reliefs and allowances is less than 1% of gross income.

    Reliefs Deductions [S 33, Schedule 6]
    • Consolidated relief allowance: Higher of N200,000 or 1% of gross income, plus 20% of gross income
    • Child allowance: N2,500 for each child up to a maximum of four children, provided that one is above 16 years or married unless in a recognised school, under articleship or learning a trade.
    • Dependent relative: N2,000 for each dependent relative up to a maximum of two who are widowed or infirmed or incapacitated by old age.
    • Deductions allowed: NHF contribution, National Health Insurance Scheme, Life Assurance Premium, National Pension Scheme and Gratuities.
    • Reimbursements: Expenses incurred in the performance of employment duties from which it is not intended that the employee should make any gain or profit.
    • Interest and dividend: Interest earned from treasury bills, government and corporate bonds are exempt while withholding tax at 10% is the final tax on other interests and dividend.
    • Interest on mortgage: Interest paid on mortgage loan for owner’s occupied property in any year is granted as a relief in the following year.
    9. PENSION CONTRIBUTION
    Relevant law

    Pension Reform Act (PRA) 2014

    Obligated persons [S 2 (1),(2),(3)]

    All employers in the public sector, and private employers with 15 or more employees.

    Rate [S 4]

    Not less than 18% of monthly emoluments* (with a minimum contribution of 10% by the employer and up to 8% by the employee). The employer and/or the employee may make additional voluntary contributions.

    Where an employer decides to solely contribute to the scheme, the contribution shall not be less than 20% of the employee’s monthly emolument.

    10. INDUSTRIAL TRAINING FUND (ITF) CONTRIBUTION
    Relevant law

    The ITF was established by Decree 47 of 1971 and amended in 2011.

    The main objective of the Fund is to generate a pool of indigenous trained manpower to meet the needs of the Nigeria economy.

    Obligated persons [S 6 (1)]

    All employers with a minimum of 25 employees or annual turnover of N50 million.

    Rate [S 6 (1)]

    1% of annual payroll cost

    11. EMPLOYEE COMPENSATION SCHEME
    Scope

    All employers, including individuals, are required to register with the Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund (NSITF) and contribute to the scheme.

    Employee compensation scheme repeals the workmen's compsensation Act and provides compensation for employees for any death, injury, disease or disabiity arising from or in the course of employment.

    Rate
    [S33(1)
    42(1)]

    1% of total monthly payroll or amount assessed by the NSITF. Where the claim cost in respect of an employer exceeds 105% of the ordinary assessment of that employer, the NSITF Board may within 4 years levy a super assessment on the employer not exceeding 133% of the ordinary assessement for the year.

    12. NATIONAL HOUSING FUND (NHF) CONTRIBUTION
    Objective

    The aims and objectives of the NHF is to provide loans to Nigerians for developing, purchasing or renovating houses and encourage housing finance among low and medium income earners.

    The Fund provides long term loans to Mortgage Institutions for lending to contributors of the Fund.

    Rate [S 4 ]

    2.5% of monthly basic salary.

    Exemptions

    Nigerians earning less than N3,000 per annum and all expatriates regardless of income level.

    13. STAMP DUTIES ACT
    Rate [ S 8 ]

    Stamp duty is tax on documents evidencing transactions between persons.

    It is chargeable either at fixed rates or ad valorem (i.e., in proportion to the value of the consideration) depending on the class of instrument.

    Instruments liable to Stamp Duty [Section 3(1)]

    All instruments relating to an act to be performed in Nigeria must be stamped, except such instrument is specifically exempted.

    14. CUSTOMS AND EXCISE DUTIES
    Relevant law

    It is governed by the Custom and Excise Management Act (CEMA) and several other Acts and Regulations relating to customs and excise matters.

    Rate

    Rates vary for different items and are assessed with reference to the prevailing rates.
